# Phone Scripts

Reference file for `va-foreclosure-case-analyzer` SKILL.md, Step 11.

Delegatable phone call scripts for household members. Each script is
3-5 bullet points maximum. Designed for someone who can make the call
but shouldn't be making decisions on it.

**Rule for the caller:** Read the script. Write down what they say.
Do NOT agree to anything. Say "I'll pass that along" if pressured.

---

## Script 1: Calling VA Hotline (877-827-3702)

**Purpose:** Check loan status, ask about VALERI records, request information.

**Say:**
- "I'm calling about VA loan number [number]. I'm the veteran / I'm
  calling on behalf of the veteran [name]."
- "Can you tell me the current status of this loan in your system?"
- "Has the servicer [servicer name] submitted any events to VALERI
  for this loan? If so, what events and on what dates?"
- "Who is the assigned VA loan technician for this loan?"

**Write down:**
- Name and ID of the person you spoke with
- Date and time of call
- Everything they say about the loan status
- Whether they confirm or deny VALERI submissions
- Name and contact info for the assigned loan technician

**Do NOT:**
- Agree to any settlement, payment plan, or release
- Provide any information beyond the loan number and borrower name
- Discuss the details of any legal claims or complaints

## Script 2: Calling the Servicer

**Purpose:** Request specific information or document a conversation.

**Say:**
- "I'm calling about loan number [number]."
- "Can I get your name and employee ID for my records?"
- "I'm calling to request [specific item: payoff statement, payment
  history, escrow analysis, status of my NOE/RFI, etc.]"
- "Can you send that to me in writing?"
- "When should I expect to receive it?"

**Write down:**
- Name and ID of the person you spoke with
- Date and time of call
- What they said about your request
- Any commitments they made (what + when)
- Any new information they provided

**Do NOT:**
- Agree to any modification, payment plan, or settlement
- Confirm or deny any facts about the case beyond the loan number
- Say "yes" or "I agree" to anything — say "I'll pass that along"
- Provide bank account information or make a payment

## Script 3: Calling VRM (Cash for Keys)

**Purpose:** Request an extension or ask questions about the Cash for Keys offer.

**Say:**
- "I'm calling about the Cash for Keys offer for [property address]."
- "I need to request an extension of the deadline. We are actively
  seeking legal counsel and need additional time."
- "Can you extend the deadline to [specific date, at least 30 days out]?"
- "If I request an extension, does that change the terms of the offer?"
- "Can you send the extension confirmation in writing?"

**Write down:**
- Name of the person you spoke with
- Date and time of call
- Whether they agreed to an extension
- New deadline if granted
- Any conditions attached to the extension

**Do NOT:**
- Sign anything or agree to any terms
- Say "we accept" or "we'll sign" — say "we're reviewing it"
- Provide information about your legal strategy or complaints filed
- Discuss the details of any violations or advocacy

## Script 4: Calling Congressional Office

**Purpose:** Follow up on a constituent inquiry or request a meeting.

**Say:**
- "I'm calling about a constituent case. My name is [name], I'm a
  [veteran / veteran's spouse] in [city, state]."
- "We submitted a request for assistance regarding a VA foreclosure
  on [date]. I'm following up on the status."
- "Has the office received a response from VA yet?"
- "We'd like to request a meeting with [staffer name or 'the staff
  member handling veterans' issues'] to discuss the case."

**Write down:**
- Name of the person you spoke with
- Case number if they assign one
- Status of the inquiry
- Next steps they recommend
- Date of any scheduled meeting or callback

**Do NOT:**
- Discuss detailed legal strategy
- Share information about media contacts or advocacy strategy
- Get frustrated with delays — be polite, note the date, follow up

## Script 5: Calling Legal Aid / Attorney Intake

**Purpose:** Inquire about representation for a VA foreclosure case.

**Say:**
- "I'm calling to inquire about possible representation for a VA
  mortgage foreclosure case."
- "The case involves a [disability rating]% disabled [branch] veteran
  in [city, state]."
- "The foreclosure has already occurred. We have documented regulatory
  violations including [1-2 most significant violations]."
- "We have a comprehensive case file prepared. Can I send an intake
  package?"
- "Do you handle VA mortgage foreclosure cases? If not, can you
  refer me to someone who does?"

**Write down:**
- Organization name and person you spoke with
- Whether they handle this type of case
- What they need from you (intake form, documents, etc.)
- Timeline for response
- Any referrals they provide

**Do NOT:**
- Send the full case file without being asked
- Agree to any fee arrangement without the primary advocate reviewing
- Share information that should be protected (strategy, media plans)

## After Every Call

1. **Write the date, time, and duration** at the top of your notes
2. **Text or tell** the primary advocate (Leann/case manager) what happened
3. **Save your notes** — phone notes are evidence
4. **Record if possible** — see call-recording-guide.md
