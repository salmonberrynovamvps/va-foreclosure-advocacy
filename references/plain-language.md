# Plain Language Style

## When to use
Outputs shared with family, friends, VSO caseworkers, community members,
faith community, or anyone who is not a legal/policy professional.
Also use for the veteran themselves if they are not the primary case
manager.

## What it optimizes for
8th-grade reading level. No jargon. Short sentences. Concrete examples.
"What this means for you" framing. Empathetic tone without being
patronizing.

## Style rules

Structure:
- Start with what the person needs to know RIGHT NOW
- Use short paragraphs — 2-3 sentences maximum
- Use bullet points for any list
- Use bold for the single most important point in each section
- No headers with legal jargon — use plain descriptions

Language:
- 8th-grade reading level or below
- No legal terms without a plain-language explanation
- No acronyms without defining them first
- Use "you/your" and "they/their" — not passive voice
- Concrete examples over abstract explanations

Tone:
- Warm and supportive
- Validate the person's experience without being clinical
- No blame, no shame, no "you should have"
- Assume the reader is stressed, scared, or overwhelmed
