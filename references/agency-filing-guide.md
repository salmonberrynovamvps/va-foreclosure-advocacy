## 1. CFPB Complaint

### Where to file

**URL:** consumerfinance.gov/complaint

### Required information

- Your name, contact information
- Company name (exact legal name of servicer)
- Loan/account number
- Product type: "Mortgage" → "VA mortgage"
- Issue category: select the closest match (e.g., "Struggling to pay
  mortgage," "Trouble during payment process")
- Narrative: detailed description of what happened

### Narrative structure

Write the CFPB narrative in this order:

1. **Who you are** — veteran status, disability rating, loan type, property
2. **What the servicer did** — chronological, specific, with dates
3. **What regulations were violated** — cite specific RESPA sections,
   CARES Act provisions
4. **What harm resulted** — financial, housing, credit, health
5. **What you want** — specific relief (correction, refund, rescission)
6. **Pattern evidence** — if the servicer has prior enforcement actions,
   note them briefly

### Tips

- **Attach documents** — the CFPB accepts attachments. Include key
  evidence (modification offer, NOE, servicer letters).
- **Keep the narrative under 2 pages** — concise and factual wins.
- **CFPB forwards to the servicer** — the servicer must respond within
  15 days. This creates a documented record.
- **Request referral** to the CFPB's Repeat Offenders Unit if the
  servicer has prior enforcement actions.

### What CFPB can do

- Forward complaint to servicer (mandatory response)
- Open supervisory investigation
- Refer to enforcement division
- Include in pattern analysis across complaints
- Refer to DOJ for fair lending violations

### What CFPB cannot do

- Represent you in court
- Order the servicer to reverse the foreclosure
- Guarantee a specific outcome

## 2. VA OIG Complaint

### Where to file

**URL:** va.gov/oig/hotline

### Required information

- Your name, contact information
- Veteran's name and relationship
- VA facility or program involved (Loan Guaranty Service)
- Loan number
- Description of the complaint
- Whether you've contacted OIG before (reference number if yes)
- Supporting documentation

### What OIG investigates

- VA-related suspected criminal activity
- Systemic patient safety issues
- **Gross mismanagement or waste of VA resources**
- Serious misconduct by senior VA officials
- Failure of VA oversight mechanisms

### Narrative structure

1. **The oversight failure** — what VA was supposed to do and didn't
   (VALERI monitoring, servicer oversight, foreclosure authorization)
2. **The regulatory violations** — 38 CFR 36.4319, M26-4 Chapter 5
3. **The evidence** — specific documents, dates, VA technician statements
4. **The systemic concern** — how this case reflects broader failures
   OIG has already documented (reference prior OIG audit findings if
   available)
5. **The ask** — supervisory review, VALERI audit, portfolio-wide
   examination of the servicer

### Supplement filings

You can supplement an existing OIG complaint with new information at any
time. Reference your existing complaint number.

## 3. State Attorney General

### Washington State

**Two possible divisions:**

| Division | What it handles | How to file |
|----------|----------------|------------|
| **OMVLA** (Office of Military & Veteran Legal Assistance) | Veteran-specific legal issues | Contact via AG's website or call |
| **Consumer Protection Division** | Unfair/deceptive practices (RCW 19.86) | File at atg.wa.gov/file-complaint |

### Narrative structure

1. **State law hooks** — lead with RCW 19.86 (CPA) and RCW 61.24 (DTA)
2. **Harm to Washington residents** — this is a state case, frame it locally
3. **Servicer's conduct** — specific violations with dates
4. **Pattern evidence** — has this servicer been cited by other state AGs?
5. **Why your state should act** — "No prior WA enforcement action against
   this servicer" = opportunity to be first

### Tips

- **State AGs care about their state's residents.** Frame everything as
  harm to Washington families, not federal regulatory violations.
- **"First to hold them accountable"** is a powerful motivator for AG
  offices looking for enforcement wins.
- **Attach your CFPB complaint** — shows you've already engaged federal
  channels.

### Adapting for other states

Search "[state name] attorney general consumer complaint" for your
state's filing portal. Most states have online complaint forms.

## 4. Credit Bureau Disputes

### Where to file

| Bureau | URL | Phone |
|--------|-----|-------|
| **Equifax** | equifax.com/personal/credit-report-services/credit-dispute | 866-349-5191 |
| **Experian** | experian.com/disputes | 888-397-3742 |
| **TransUnion** | transunion.com/credit-disputes | 800-916-8800 |

### What to dispute

- Delinquencies reported during CARES Act forbearance period
- Backdated derogatory marks into protected periods
- Duplicate/phantom tradelines
- Incorrect balance, payment history, or account status
- Foreclosure reporting (if foreclosure was unauthorized)

### Dispute letter structure

1. **Identify yourself** — name, address, last 4 of SSN, date of birth
2. **Identify the account** — servicer name, account number
3. **Identify the specific error** — which reporting is inaccurate, for
   which months
4. **Cite the legal basis** — FCRA §623, CARES Act §4022
5. **State what you want corrected** — specific months to be marked
   current, tradeline to be removed, etc.
6. **Attach evidence** — forbearance agreement, payment history,
   servicer correspondence

### File with ALL THREE bureaus

Each bureau maintains its own file. An error corrected at Equifax may
still appear at Experian and TransUnion. File separately with each.

### Timeline

- Bureau must investigate within **30 days** of receiving dispute
- Bureau must notify the furnisher (servicer)
- Furnisher must investigate and respond
- If the furnisher cannot verify the disputed information, it must be
  deleted

## 5. FOIA Request to VA

### Where to file

**URL:** department-level FOIA office varies. For Loan Guaranty records,
submit to the VA Regional Loan Center (RLC) that services your area.

**VA FOIA portal:** va.gov/foia

### What to request

```
Under the Freedom of Information Act (5 U.S.C. §552), I request
copies of the following records related to VA Loan #[number]:

1. All VALERI records for this loan, including but not limited to:
   - Servicer-submitted events and their dates
   - Loss mitigation status entries
   - Foreclosure authorization records
   - Infraction or exception flags
   - All internal notes and correspondence

2. All correspondence between VA and [servicer name] regarding
   this loan

3. All internal VA memoranda, emails, or notes regarding this
   loan or the borrower(s)

4. Records of any VA review or audit of [servicer name]'s
   servicing of this loan

I request a fee waiver under 5 U.S.C. §552(a)(4)(A)(iii) as
disclosure is in the public interest and I am not seeking records
for commercial purposes. I am a [veteran/veteran's spouse] seeking
records related to my own VA-guaranteed loan.

If any records are withheld, please identify the specific
exemption(s) claimed and provide any reasonably segregable
non-exempt portions.
```

### Tips

- **Be specific** — vague requests take longer and may be denied
- **Request fee waiver** — veteran requestors seeking their own records
  have strong grounds
- **VA response timeline:** 20 business days (often much longer in practice)
- **Follow up** if no response in 30 days — reference your request number

## 6. Qualified Written Request to Servicer

### What it is

Under RESPA §2605(e), a **Qualified Written Request (QWR)** is a written
request that the servicer must respond to within 30 business days.

### What to request

```
This is a Qualified Written Request under the Real Estate
Settlement Procedures Act, 12 U.S.C. §2605(e).

Borrower: [name]
Loan number: [number]
Property: [address]

I request the following information and documents:

1. Complete payment history from loan origination to present,
   including application of each payment (principal, interest,
   escrow, fees)

2. Complete escrow account history, including annual analyses
   and all disbursements

3. All loss mitigation applications submitted by the borrower,
   servicer evaluations, and decision letters

4. Complete record of VALERI submissions made by [servicer]
   for this loan

5. All internal notes, memoranda, or communications regarding
   this loan

6. Itemization of all fees charged to this account

7. All documents submitted to the foreclosure trustee or
   attorney regarding this loan

Please respond within 30 business days as required by
12 CFR §1024.36. Failure to respond constitutes a violation
of RESPA.
```

### How to send

- **Certified mail, return receipt requested** — this proves delivery
- Send to the address designated for qualified written requests (check
  servicer's website or monthly statement for the correct address —
  it may differ from the general mailing address)
- Keep a copy of everything you send

## 7. Congressional Inquiry

### How it works

Congressional offices have a **constituent services** function. When a
constituent contacts their representative or senator about a problem
with a federal agency, the office can submit a formal inquiry to the
agency on the constituent's behalf.

### What to provide

- Brief summary of the issue (1 page maximum)
- Your contact information
- Signed **privacy release** (the office will provide their form)
- Key documents (advocacy brief, timeline, violation summary)

### What the congressional office does

- Submits a formal inquiry to VA (through OCLA — Office of Congressional
  & Legislative Affairs)
- VA must respond to congressional inquiries (though response times
  vary — currently averaging 105 business days through OCLA)
- The inquiry creates a documented record within VA

### Tips

- **Start with your own representative/senators** — they have the
  strongest constituent obligation
- **Committee staff** (HVAC, Senate VAC) handle policy/oversight — they
  can request broader action, not just individual case resolution
- **Provide the brief, not the full file** — congressional staff are
  busy. Give them the one-pager and offer to provide more on request.

## 8. Filing Strategy and Sequencing

### What to file first

| Priority | Filing | Why first |
|----------|--------|----------|
| 1 | **CFPB complaint** | Creates a documented record; servicer must respond in 15 days; builds pattern evidence in CFPB database |
| 2 | **Credit bureau disputes** | 30-day investigation timeline starts; protects credit while other filings proceed |
| 3 | **VA OIG supplement** (if already filed) | Adds new evidence to existing investigation |
| 4 | **Congressional inquiry** | Takes longest to produce results; start early |
| 5 | **State AG complaint** | Can reference CFPB complaint as supporting evidence |
| 6 | **FOIA to VA** | 20+ business day timeline; results feed back into all other filings |
| 7 | **QWR to servicer** | 30-day timeline; results may reveal new violations |

### Sequencing principles

- **SOL-urgent filings go first** — regardless of strategic timing
- **CFPB before state AG** — reference the CFPB complaint in the AG filing
- **Congressional before or after CFPB?** Both work. Congressional can
  pressure VA to respond faster. CFPB complaint gives the congressional
  office something concrete to reference.
- **FOIA early** — results take time and feed into everything else
- **Media coordination** — if a reporter is working on a story, time
  complaint filings to maximize investigative impact. But never hold
  a SOL-urgent filing for media timing.

### What filing does NOT require an attorney

- CFPB complaint (online form)
- VA OIG complaint (online form)
- Credit bureau disputes (online or mail)
- State AG complaint (online form)
- FOIA request (letter or online)
- Congressional inquiry (phone or email)
- QWR to servicer (letter)

All of the above can be done pro se. The skill helps draft all of them.

### What DOES require an attorney

- Filing a lawsuit
- Challenging the foreclosure sale in court
- Negotiating a settlement with legal releases
- Asserting claims in a bankruptcy proceeding
