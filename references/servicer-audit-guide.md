# Servicer Enforcement History Audit Guide

Reference file for `va-foreclosure-case-analyzer` SKILL.md, Step 9.

How to research a mortgage servicer's pattern of conduct across federal
and state enforcement databases. Results transform an individual
complaint into evidence of a corporate pattern.

---

## Why This Matters

**Individual complaint:** "This servicer violated RESPA on my loan."

**Pattern complaint:** "This servicer has $130M in cumulative penalties
across 18 enforcement actions, was designated a CFPB repeat offender,
and the same escrow and loss mitigation failures alleged here appear
in consent orders from three other states."

Agencies investigate patterns. Build the pattern.

---

## Search Checklist

Work through these sources in order. Document findings in the summary
template at the bottom.

---

### 1. CFPB Enforcement Actions

**URL:** consumerfinance.gov/enforcement/actions

**Search for:** Servicer's legal name and any parent/subsidiary names.

**What to look for:**
- Consent orders
- Civil money penalties
- Supervisory actions
- Repeat offender designations
- Specific violations cited (compare to your case)

---

### 2. CFPB Complaint Database

**URL:** consumerfinance.gov/data-research/consumer-complaints

**Search for:** Company name, filter by "Mortgage" product.

**What to look for:**
- Total complaint volume
- Most common complaint types
- Geographic distribution
- Company response patterns ("Closed with explanation" vs. "Closed
  with relief")
- Complaints describing the same conduct you experienced

---

### 3. State Attorney General Actions

**How to search:** Google "[servicer name] attorney general settlement"
or "[servicer name] state AG enforcement"

**Key states to check:**
- Your state (most relevant)
- NJ, CA, NY, IL, FL, TX (most active AG offices)
- Any state where the servicer is headquartered

**What to look for:**
- Settlement amounts and terms
- Conduct cited (compare to your case)
- Whether the servicer was required to change practices
- Whether the servicer complied with prior settlements

---

### 4. HMDA Data

**URL:** ffiec.cfpb.gov/data-browser

**Search for:** Servicer name, filter by geography, loan type (VA).

**What to look for:**
- Denial rates by race/ethnicity compared to industry average
- Foreclosure rates by demographic (if available)
- Lending patterns suggesting fair lending concerns
- Disparities in loss mitigation outcomes by demographic

---

### 5. Ginnie Mae / VA Restrictions

**How to search:** Google "[servicer name] Ginnie Mae restriction" or
"[servicer name] VA churning"

**What to look for:**
- Restrictions on VA loan origination (churning penalties)
- Ginnie Mae issuer restrictions
- VA sanctions or warnings
- Servicing transfer orders

---

### 6. Federal Court Filings (PACER)

**URL:** pacer.uscourts.gov (requires account, small per-page fee)

**Alternative:** Google "[servicer name] class action" or search
courtlistener.com (free)

**What to look for:**
- Class action lawsuits alleging similar conduct
- Individual lawsuits with published opinions
- Settlement agreements (may be public)
- Pattern litigation in multiple districts

---

### 7. BBB (Better Business Bureau)

**URL:** bbb.org

**Search for:** Servicer name.

**What to look for:**
- BBB accreditation status (revoked = significant)
- Complaint volume and patterns
- Company response to complaints
- Rating and trend

---

### 8. Congressional Testimony

**How to search:** Google "[servicer name] congressional hearing" or
"[servicer name] testimony"

**What to look for:**
- Has the servicer testified before Congress?
- What did they promise?
- Did they follow through?
- Have members of Congress criticized this servicer publicly?
- Admissions against interest in testimony

---

### 9. News / Investigative Reporting

**How to search:** Google News for "[servicer name] foreclosure" or
"[servicer name] violations" or "[servicer name] penalty"

**What to look for:**
- Investigative stories documenting patterns
- Borrower interviews describing similar experiences
- Industry analysis of the servicer's practices

---

## Enforcement History Summary Template

Fill this out after completing the search:

```
SERVICER ENFORCEMENT HISTORY SUMMARY

Company: ________________________________
Legal name: ________________________________
Headquarters: ________________________________
VA portfolio size: ________ loans / $________ UPB
Total penalties found: $________________________________
Number of enforcement actions: ________________________________
CFPB repeat offender designation: YES / NO
BBB accreditation status: ________________________________

ENFORCEMENT ACTIONS (chronological)

# | Year | Agency | Violation type | Penalty | Same conduct as our case?
1 | ____ | ______ | ______________ | $______ | YES / NO / PARTIAL
2 | ____ | ______ | ______________ | $______ | YES / NO / PARTIAL
3 | ____ | ______ | ______________ | $______ | YES / NO / PARTIAL
[continue]

CONDUCT OVERLAP ANALYSIS

Violations in our case that appear in prior enforcement actions:
- [violation] → appeared in [year] [agency] action
- [violation] → appeared in [year] [agency] action
- [violation] → no prior match found

Violations in prior actions that suggest additional issues to investigate:
- [conduct from prior action] → check whether this also occurred here

PATTERN ASSESSMENT

□ Isolated incident (no prior similar conduct)
□ Emerging pattern (1-2 prior similar actions)
□ Established pattern (3+ prior similar actions)
□ Repeat offender (designated by CFPB or subject to consent order
  requiring practice changes that were not made)

ADVOCACY VALUE

This enforcement history is most powerful for:
□ CFPB complaint (pattern evidence, repeat offender)
□ State AG complaint ("your state could be next")
□ Congressional advocacy (corporate accountability frame)
□ Media (documented pattern, dollar amounts)
□ Attorney intake (establishes pattern for CPA treble damages)
```
